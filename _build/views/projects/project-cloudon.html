Mobile Collaboration and Productivity

Personas

Modelling our users

In order to be able to determine some of the personality traits and motivations of our users we created personas and mapped them to user stories that we could use to achieve our users' goals.

Nav Model Redesign

Moving to a metadata driven information model

Findings through RITE gave us immediate visibility around the navigational challenges facing the users. At this point we started looking at other patterns that would be consistently effective across all device modalities and we found the drill down pattern struck a chord with our users in initial walk throughs. At this point we worked our way up from the smartphone, to the tablet and finally to the web app through responsive prototypes that we used for validations at each step.

Cross Device Consistency

Maintaining cross device consistency

Cross device consistency was attained through the creation of parallel flows covering all entry points, in flows, out flows and respecting device specific idioms across scenarios with the help of personas and validated with prototypes in multi device testing scenarios. These were done remotely and contextually.

People Space

Adding Collaboration

Visualizing future feature areas was another concern early on that we approached through wireframes and cognitive walkthroughs before then diving in with html/js prototypes that we could then use for evaluation and testing. Through our content object models, we already knew that we were going to need three distinct modalities for collaboration and production to take place, so we planned early on by experimenting with people, file and work space idioms.

Quality of Experience

Error flow

Quality was also a very critical area for us as we provided a virtualization and document management service to our users through their data providers which led to a multitude of scenarios we had to account for. From connectivity issues to cognitive temporal issues, these scenarios required us to determine intent as accurately as possible and then respond with the correct affect through our voice and tone guidelines work that came out of our personas.

Prototyping New Features

Notifications Mockup and Prototype

As we started adding more features across devices we found that prototyping them in html/js was the best way to experiment, test and communicate a particular interaction model and its rulesets.

CloudOn Web

Closing the loop with Web

In order to have a friction less experience for our users across all devices we had to ensure that a desktop and mobile web existed so that non logged in users working on desktop could also work with those using our app. This was one of our main growth strategies as we wanted to maintain a small subset of features as freemium features in order to enable growth wherever we could, without pushing it on anyone.

Redesigning a tablet application across all devices

As the Product Design Lead it was my primary responsibility to set up a more user centric design process and build a team in order to start tackling a major redesign of our tablet application across all devices.
Initially, we needed to start creating task and UI analysis of the current features and map those to their user stories. Once we managed to articulate our current processes and features we were able to start measuring and modelling them with various qualitative data points on hand. This gave us a good place from where we could start asking the right questions and in turn start bringing in users for testing and interviews.
I decided to take a mobile first approach in order to focus our features and streamline our flows on our tablet device by constraining ourselves to both the device and rigid attention to feature validation by the users. We immediately noticed that we had huge navigational and cognitive issues that required restructuring our navigation model to something like a drill down pattern based on multiple findings from usability tests, interviews and contextual inquiries done with various prototypes.
